The results in one paragraph

For any finite black configuration, it is conjectured that after some time the ant’s future path and turn trace agree with the period-104 highway, up to lattice translation, quarter-turn rotation, and cyclic phase; inert debris outside the future path may remain. We prove that any finite-support periodic highway must satisfy strong constraints: it cannot be a zero-growth glider (the even-winding theorem), so it always grows a wake of net size a positive multiple of four; its checker-signed wake bases obey an exact mod-four residue identity, giving the bound g ≥ 2 max(|a|,|b|) between growth and drift; the dynamics is exactly conjugate to edge-toggling on a Tait graph with a cycle-rank surgery law. For diagonal drift, extremal-line rigidity gives permanent guard rails; transverse widths two and four are impossible at every period. The width-four theorem uses a 12-edge blank-column graph certified by two independent exact enumerators, with its rank consequence checked in Lean. Finally, an exhaustive residue-theorem-free, rank-audited computation shows there is no finite-support periodic highway of nonzero drift and period ≤ 48. Selected algebraic kernels are machine-checked in Lean 4; the complete geometric theorems are not formalized end to end.

What remains open

  • A bounded-active-core lemma: after translation, one finite window must contain the ant and every cell or Tait-boundary component capable of influencing the future; everything outside it must be permanently inert.
  • A single-tour chronology theorem classifying positive-growth periodic traces for unbounded period, including widths six and eight and non-diagonal drift.

These, and not the arithmetic constraints proved here, are what a full solution of the conjecture must still supply.
