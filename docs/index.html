The results in one paragraph

Langton’s ant, from any finite black configuration, is conjectured to eventually build the period-104 “highway.” We prove that any finite-support periodic highway must satisfy strong constraints: it cannot be a zero-growth glider (the even-winding theorem), so it always grows a wake of net size a positive multiple of four; its checker-signed wake bases obey an exact mod-four residue identity, giving the sharp bound g ≥ 2 max(|a|,|b|) between growth and drift; the dynamics is exactly conjugate to edge-toggling on a Tait graph with a cycle-rank surgery law; and an exhaustive two-engine, rank-audited computation shows there is no periodic highway of period ≤ 48 other than the standard one. The algebraic core is machine-checked in Lean 4.

What remains open

  • A bounded-retreat / bounded-active-core lemma: that the ant eventually stays in a bounded corridor behind its frontier.
  • A single-tour chronology theorem classifying positive-growth periodic traces for unbounded period.

These, and not the arithmetic constraints proved here, are what a full solution of the conjecture must still supply.
