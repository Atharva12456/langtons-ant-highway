Finite-Support Periodic Highways of Langton’s Ant: Necessary Conditions, Transverse Exclusions, and Exact Search

Atharva Jillhewar · 21 July 2026

Status: the highway conjecture remains open. This work proves necessary conditions for periodic highways and an exact finite-period exclusion; it does not prove that an arbitrary finite configuration’s future path and turn trace agree with the standard highway, nor that the standard period-104 trace is the only possible periodic trace. The conjecture permits stationary debris outside the future path and does not assert equality of the entire colouring.

The paper gives a decidable finite-support periodic-realisability criterion, an even-winding theorem, a signed mod-four wake-residue identity, a Tait-graph surgery description, and a two-endpoint collision parity theorem. Audited residue-theorem-free search records exclude nonzero-drift periods at most 48. Selected algebraic kernels, but not the complete dynamical theorems, are checked in Lean 4.

New in this version. A transverse rigidity theorem: for diagonal drift the two extremal level lines of a periodic highway share one arrival axis, every turn on them is R, and each of their cells the trace reaches is entered exactly once and never revisited — the highway runs between two guard rails of permanent wake, and its transverse width is even. From this follows the exclusion of transverse width two. A computer-assisted crossing-sequence theorem also excludes width four at every period: two independent exact enumerators reproduce the complete 12-edge blank-column graph, and Lean verifies that every edge lowers rank. Hence a diagonal periodic highway has even transverse width at least six. Completeness of the local enumeration is not formalized in Lean, and widths six/eight remain open.
